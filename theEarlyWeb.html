The Early Web

What Happened to the early web. When I was a little kid using the internet I remember the crazy amounts of customization the early web gave its users. We used to be able to edit the web contents to our liking without needing approval. Now I am not naive. I get it, the internet today is not only much more complicated, but also mobile! what used to require a desktop, an internet connection & your full focus, now merely requires a smartphone with an app store. Its safe to say that the app store killed the web. Now, will this be permanent or will this be a time thing, im not sure. But what can be said is that apps do offer more control over the user experience as opposed to a webpage. But what is missing in the app experience is the freedom to navigate to other webpages. The whole point of the "In App Experience" is that it is a controlled environment. A walled garden. Now listen, I love flowers & I love Apples. Im not opposed to building controlled ecosystems. But closing in & out of apps is such a hassle. I imagine a future where apps "communicate" with each other effortlessly. What this looks like im not sure, but what I do know is that the current app system is not only stifling competition, but also innovation. These apps are getting better & more optimized, but that cross app communication is missing.
